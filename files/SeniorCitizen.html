Senior Citizen

In India, there are specific laws and regulations aimed at protecting and promoting the rights and welfare of senior citizens. Some of the key laws and initiatives related to senior citizens in India include:

Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, 2007

This act provides legal provisions for the maintenance and welfare of senior citizens, including the obligation of children and heirs to provide financial support to their elderly parents.

National Council for Older Persons (NCOP)

Established under the Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizens Act, NCOP works to address issues related to senior citizens, coordinate activities, and provide recommendations for their well-being.

Pension Schemes

The Indian government offers various pension schemes for senior citizens, including the National Social Assistance Programme (NSAP). https://nsap.nic.in/ The Indira Gandhi National Old Age Pension Scheme (IGNOAPS) https://angul.nic.in/service/indira-gandhi-national-old-age-pension-scheme-ignoaps/

Health Care

The government has initiated programs like the National Program for Healthcare of the Elderly (NPHCE) to provide healthcare services and promote the well-being of senior citizens. https://main.mohfw.gov.in/?q=major-programmes/Non-Communicable-Diseases/Non-Communicable-Diseases-1

Concessions and Benefits

Various concessions and benefits are provided to senior citizens, including reduced railway fares, income tax benefits, and reserved seats on public transportation.

Senior Citizen Associations

Many states and cities have senior citizen associations and forums that offer support, organize activities, and represent the interests of older individuals.

Legal Aid and Protection

Senior citizens can seek legal aid and protection through initiatives like the Senior Citizens' Welfare Committee, which aims to address legal issues affecting senior citizens.

The Maintenance and Welfare of Parents and Senior Citizen Act, 2007

It was enacted by Parliament to provide financial security, welfare and protection to senior citizens.

Atal Pension Yojna

It was launched to create a universal social security system for all Indians, especially the poor, the under-privileged and the workers in the unorganized sector. APY is administered by Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA). It is open to all bank account holders in the age group of 18 to 40 years and the contributions differ, based on pension amount chosen. https://financialservices.gov.in/pension-reforms-divisions/Atal-Pension-Yojana
