LGBTQ+

The legal landscape for LGBTQ+ rights in India has evolved significantly in recent years. Here are key laws and developments related to LGBTQ+ rights in India:

Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code

In a historic judgment in 2018, the Indian Supreme Court decriminalized consensual same-sex relations by partially striking down Section 377, which criminalized "unnatural offenses." This ruling upheld the rights of LGBTQ+ individuals to live without fear of persecution.

NALSA Judgment

The National Legal Services Authority (NALSA) judgment in 2014 recognized the rights of transgender individuals and established the legal recognition of a third gender. It called for protections and affirmative action for transgender persons.

Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Act, 2019

This legislation was enacted to protect the rights of transgender individuals and ensure their social, economic, and educational inclusion. It provides for self-identification of gender and prohibits discrimination against transgender persons.

Anti-Discrimination Laws

Various state governments in India have taken steps to protect LGBTQ+ individuals from discrimination by amending their labor laws to include sexual orientation as a protected category.

Progress in Marriage Rights

While same-sex marriage is not yet legally recognized at the national level, there have been legal challenges and discussions regarding marriage equality for LGBTQ+ couples. Some individual states and jurisdictions have shown support for such rights.
