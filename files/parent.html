Arresting a Child

  • The police cannot handcuff or exert any force when arresting a child.
  • The police officer must immediately inform the parents or guardians.
  • The police officer must inform the child about the location of the Juvenile Justice Board where they will be taken.

If a child in India is arrested, parents should seek legal services to protect their child's rights. They can consult with a qualified juvenile defense lawyer to navigate the legal process, understand the charges, and ensure a fair trial. The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 is the primary legal framework for juvenile cases in India. Parents can find more information on this topic on the website of the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) at https://ncpcr.gov.in, and consult with legal professionals to explore their specific case further.
